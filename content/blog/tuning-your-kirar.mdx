Before you play a single scale, your Kirar needs to be in tune — and because
the Kirar doesn't use fixed frets, tuning is a skill in itself, not a
one-time setup step.

## Start with your reference note

Pick one string as your reference — often the lowest — and tune the rest of
the instrument relative to it. Consistency matters more than hitting an
absolute pitch, especially early on: what matters most is that the
intervals between strings are correct.

## Small adjustments, frequent checks

The Kirar's strings will drift, especially when the instrument is new or the
weather changes. Get in the habit of checking tuning every time you sit down
to practice, rather than assuming yesterday's tuning still holds.

## Tune to the mode you're playing

Because traditional Ethiopian modes like Tizita have their own characteristic
intervals, many players adjust their tuning slightly depending on what
they're about to play, rather than tuning once to a fixed Western reference.

## It gets faster

Tuning by ear feels slow at first. It won't stay that way — within a few
weeks of regular practice, most students tune in under a minute. This is one
of the very first things we cover, hands-on, in the Beginner course.
